The problem

San Diego County will need to house 1,000,000 more residents by 2050*

* Source: San Diego Associciation of Governments

Current housing projections by San Diego Association of Governments (SANDAG) show that San Diego County will need to house one million more residents by 2050, which equates to approximately 350,000 new housing units. Facing an expensive housing market with such a critical shortage of affordable housing, the Tiny Home Subcommittee and AIA San Diego (AIA SD) have teamed up to demonstrate how tiny homes and modular housing can serve as alternative housing solutions to address this real problem. This solution is not a one-size-fits-all approach, but rather an additional methodology to help address and resolve the housing crisis facing current and future residents of San Diego County.

Our proposal

Use tiny home communities as an efficient, affordable living situation that residents can benefit from and be proud of

Why tiny homes and modular construction? With their increase in popularity, tiny homes and modular units have been the topic of countless articles and discussions about how living a simplified lifestyle can increase health outcomes. The typical solutions for affordable housing are multistory high-occupancy structures, which require extensive planning and development costs. Tiny homes and modular housing units can also allow for the creation of small communities with lower development costs, foster independence and positive decision-making of one’s own surroundings and bolster an individual’s pride given their autonomous home occupancy.

The event

Our goal with the event is to educate and inspire members of community with our proposal

Location: Jacobs Center for Neighborhood Innovation, Market Creek Plaza
Address: 310 Euclid Ave, San Diego, CA 92114

In a partnership between the Tiny Home Subcommittee and the San Diego Chapter of the American Institute of Architects (AIA), this 2-and-a-half day demonstration event will offer community members, key decision-makers, and potential clients an in-person, life-size and hands-on experience to learn about these smaller structures. We intend for this event to encourage dialogue, to inspire innovation, and to expand the perception of what a healthy home environment can be.

About us

The Tiny Home Subcommittee

The Tiny Home Subcommittee is made up of independent representatives from social service nonprofits, mental health agencies, community members, and mental health peers. Although reporting to the San Diego County Behavioral Health Housing Council, all positions taken are our own and are not meant to speak for staff or members of County Behavioral Health Services. Our main purpose is to investigate the potential opportunities tiny homes can allow for those with behavioral health challenges given our current housing shortage and lack of affordable housing options.

The Tiny Homes subcommittee is tasked with the following:

  1. Set up demonstration project, 3-4 tiny home units that the community would be able to tour.
  2. Work on plans for a permanent tiny home village pilot project
