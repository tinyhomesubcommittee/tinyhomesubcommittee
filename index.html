The problem

San Diego County will need to house 1,000,000 more residence by 2050*

* Source: San Diego Associciation of Governments

Current housing projections by San Diego Association of Governments (SANDAG) show that San Diego County will need to house one million more residents by 2050, which equates to approximately 350,000 new housing units. Facing an expensive housing market with such a critical shortage of affordable housing, the SD County Behavioral Health Housing Council (SDC BHHC) and AIA San Diego (AIA SD) have teamed up to demonstrate how tiny homes and modular housing can serve as alternative housing solutions to address this real problem. This solution is not a one-size-fits-all approach, but rather an additional methodology to help address and resolve the housing crisis facing current and future residents of San Diego County.

Our proposal

Use tiny house communities as an efficient, affordable living situation that residents can benefit from and be proud of

Why tiny homes and modular construction? With their increase in popularity, tiny homes and modular units have been the topic of countless articles and discussions about how living a simplified lifestyle can increase health outcomes. The typical solutions for affordable housing are multistory high-occupancy structures, which require extensive planning and development costs. Tiny homes and modular housing units can also allow for the creation of small communities with lower development costs, foster independence and positive decision-making of one’s own surroundings and bolster an individual’s pride given their autonomous home occupancy.
